## Search Objective
- **Primary outcome:** land a senior or principal product design role
- **Search mode:** active
- **Decision standard:** prioritize roles with strategic scope, product influence, and clear ownership

## Target Role Set
### Primary roles
- **Senior Product Designer**
- **Principal Product Designer**
- **Lead Product Designer**

### Secondary roles
- **Staff Product Designer**
- **Design Lead**
- **Founding Product Designer**

### Deprioritized roles
- execution-only design roles
- production-heavy UI roles with low product influence
- senior-titled roles with low ownership
- visually oriented roles without systems or strategy scope

## Positioning Model
- **Core positioning:** strategic product designer with systems thinking, clarity-building ability, and strong execution translation
- **Narrative emphasis:** operates well in ambiguity; improves product clarity, decision quality, and cross-functional execution
- **Market message:** more than interface craft; valuable at the level of framing, prioritization, and operational leverage

## Strengths To Lead With
- **Systems thinking**
  - structures ambiguity into coherent operating models
- **Strategic range**
  - connects design choices to product direction and business leverage
- **Clarity creation**
  - useful in environments with weak prioritization or messy problem spaces
- **Execution translation**
  - turns abstract goals into concrete artifacts and next moves
- **Cross-functional value**
  - strongest where design must shape product thinking, not just outputs

## Gaps To Close
- **Portfolio proof**
  - show strategic ownership and measurable impact more clearly
- **Role narrative**
  - tighten mapping between experience and senior/principal role expectations
- **Pipeline rigor**
  - maintain visible opportunity tracking and next actions
- **Interview stories**
  - prepare concise evidence of scope, influence, and decision quality
- **Fit filtering**
  - avoid spending energy on low-leverage roles

## Market Wedge
- **Best-fit role condition:** teams facing ambiguity, scale, or product complexity
- **Best-fit company condition:** companies that want design to shape product direction
- **Best-fit narrative:** designer of product clarity and systems, not only screens and flows

## Decision Filters
### Strong-fit signals
- design has strategic input
- role includes problem framing and product collaboration
- ambiguity is explicit in the role
- ownership goes beyond UI delivery

### Weak-fit signals
- design operates as service-only support
- role language centers on output, polish, or tickets
- strategic ownership is absent
- title inflation without matching scope
