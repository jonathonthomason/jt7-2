# JT7-2 Cockpit Strategy Note

## Audience
Product, strategy, marketing, future persona work

## What JT7-2 Is
JT7-2 is an operator cockpit for job-search execution.

It is not just a dashboard and not just an inbox.
It is a working environment that:
- receives signals
- helps an operator verify what is real
- promotes verified items into trusted opportunity state
- preserves action history
- supports future intelligence

## Core Product Idea
Signals are reviewed.
Opportunities are operated.
Intelligence learns.

That is the clearest product framing for the cockpit.

## Why This Matters
Most job-search systems either:
- dump raw inbound noise into a tracker, or
- act like static dashboards detached from source evidence

JT7-2 should do neither.

The product advantage is the trust boundary:
- raw signals are untrusted on arrival
- operator review determines whether they become real opportunities
- dashboard/pipeline views operate on trusted state only

## Product Roles
### JT7
Builds and maintains the cockpit platform.

### JobOps
Eventually operates inside the cockpit as the pilot handling daily workflow.

### End-user mental model
- JT7 = engineer/platform builder
- JobOps = pilot/operator

## First Product Surface
### Review Queue
The first meaningful product surface is the Review Queue because it defines how truth enters the system.

Without it, the rest of the cockpit inherits ambiguity and noise.

## What Makes The Cockpit Valuable
### 1. Trustworthy state
The dashboard reflects verified opportunities rather than raw intake.

### 2. Source traceability
Every opportunity, signal, and contact retains a path back to source evidence.

### 3. Operational usability
The operator can move directly from summary to source, application, recruiter context, and next action.

### 4. Intelligence readiness
The system captures not only what happened, but what the operator decided.

## Product Rule
The dashboard is not the inbox.
The cockpit includes intake, review, and operations.
The dashboard is only one surface inside that cockpit.

## Future Messaging Implication
For product/marketing audiences, JT7-2 should be described less as:
- a job tracker
- an AI dashboard

and more as:
- an operator cockpit for job-search execution
- a trust-aware system that turns noisy signals into actionable opportunities
- a platform that learns from operator behavior over time

## Strategic Outcome
This framing supports three audiences cleanly:
- engineering: trust boundary, event model, source-linking, surface architecture
- product: workflow clarity and handoff model
- marketing/persona: operator cockpit, not passive dashboard
